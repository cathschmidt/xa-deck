The Thesis

1

Economies compute value.

  • Ruled by numbers: Economic processes are fundamentally computational.
  • Networks compute: They coordinate according to a formal logic; a network language that defines what value is and how it is calculated, economic protocols.tooltip text
  • Distributing power: These protocols dictate how value is recorded, calculated, understood and utilized. The network computations they enable determine the outcomes, limitations, and agency given to participants.
  • Understanding this is key to guide their evolution.

Economic space agency is the power to shape—and benefit from—economic protocols.

2

Economic computation is evolving.

From tablets to blockchains, a short history of innovation in economic computation.

  What it is What it enabled Limitation
Tablet & Tally Stick Computation (~3000 BCE) a shared state. A durable memory for economic agreements, moving logic from the individual mind to a persistent external object. Durable record & basic syntax. It established a formal syntax for accounting (e.g., this many sheep for this much grain), enabling economic trust and coordination to extend beyond immediate personal relationships and memory. Local & static. Computation was manual and confined to the physical object. The logic was local to the specific record-keepers and could not dynamically coordinate across a wide network.
Double-Entry Ledger Computation (~1500s) A structured grammar of accounts (assets, liabilities, equity) that formalized a coherent, self-balancing system for tracking flows of value. Systemic consistency & capital calculation. It created a robust system for tracking flows and for calculating a singular, system-wide surplus (profit). This grammar became the operating system for modern capitalism. Centralized & opaque logic. The ledger remained controlled by a central entity (a firm, a bank). The economic logic was still executed locally. There was no native way to compute dynamic flows between independent ledgers.
Market Computation (~1900s) A distributed system where independent agents, each with a local view, interact through the price signal. Emergent network coordination at scale. For the first time, the network itself began to compute - the network itself became a computer - generating a system-wide signal (price) that coordinated vast numbers of actors without a central planner. Opaque & narrow grammar. The network's computational logic was an opaque "black box" that agents could only react to, not consciously design. It reduced all value to a single price dimension and couldn't express richer relationships.
Platform Computation (~2000s) A central actor aggregating network-wide data to coordinate participants. Centralized network awareness. The platform operator solved the opacity problem of markets by achieving a privileged, "god-like" view of the entire network, allowing for sophisticated, network-wide optimization. Extractive & dictatorial grammar. This awareness was proprietary and used to impose an extractive economic logic. The network's grammar was dictated by a single actor optimizing for its own profit, not the utility of the participants.
Blockchain Computation (~2010s) A distributed system for collectively owning and executing changes to a shared, public state record without a central operator. Trustless state consensus. It proved a network could enforce its own rules - a syntax could be distributed - and achieve consensus on its state, a monumental leap in computational trust and decentralizing the substrate. Centralized & inexpressive economic grammar. While decentralizing state replication, blockchain re-centralizes economic meaning around a rigid, monolithic grammar ("one ledger, one coin"), struggling to express economic-organizationally anything else. In other words, blockchains solved centralization of state but not of economic logic and agency.
Distributed Economic Computation (NOW) A new paradigm of computation where the entire economic grammar—the power to define, compute, and govern value—is distributed to the network's edge. Economic network semantics that distributes all the protocol layers (state, computation, value definition, governance) to the network’s edge. Composable and interoperable economics = Internet-native agency = Distributed economic intelligence: The network itself gains the power not just to be seen (like a blockchain), but to define and compute its own multi-dimensional utility and value. Network agents become active authors of their economic reality. has not yet been formalized as a protocol. This task is at the core of the ecsa project. We think it will result in an unprecedented financial and political opportunity: the economic space agency spread.
